About me

A little over 2 years back, I decided to change my career path towards the Data Science field. Before making such a decision, my research led me to a single question: “What would you do for free?” This question was the key to my decision since it forced me to remember experiences I had throughout my life where I felt fulfilled. I remember me seeking data on the internet looking for answers that I could not find them easily. Listing a few examples, I remember spending hours in the US census data to find out how poverty was spread in the US and if it was associated with race, population density, or if there was any relationship with education. I remember diving into the lottery past results and creating a spreadsheet with the use of descriptive statistics to help me to find the best numbers. After realizing a series of accidents happening close to home, I reached out Department of Public Safety to ask if they could release traffic and accidents data. My question was literally if there was a way to lower the number of accidents by looking at the data. When I concluded my research, I realized that everything I did was for fun, in fact, I always enjoyed finding out hidden information and bringing them to discussions with friends. In the end, I have been behaving as a data scientist or data appreciator for years, and knowing it became a profession was just what I needed to make my decision.

Projects

Linear Regression

Created a pricing model that effectively predicted the price of used car in the Indian Market and helped the company develop profitable strategies using differential pricing. If the company knows the market price, it will never sell anything below it. R-square of 86% and MAE around 1.4 small, Indian currency data set.

Classification, Capstone Project

Created a classification model to identify customers satisfaction based on flight survey performed by an airline company. Dataset of 2M data points. Technics were used such as data type optimization, principal components analysis (PCA), KNN for missing value input, data transformation, down sampling

Ensemble Methods & Model tuning

Performed univariate, bivariate and multivariate exploratory data analysis on population data set to understand customer segmentation. Created a model to predict whether a customer would churn or not. Used Logistic Regression, Random Forest Classifier, Bagging Classifiers, Boosting Classifiers, and visualized results in confusion matrix layout, maximized recall score above 93% and accuracy score above 91%

Ensemble Methods

Built machine learning models to predict whether a travel agency customer would buy a new travel package or not. Redirected the marketing campaign and reduced costs. Used Bagging Classifiers, Boosting Classifiers and Stacking Classifiers, visualized results in confusion matrix layout, maximized precision score 75%, correctly predicting 86.5% of customers would buy or not the offered package

Business Statistics

Developed exploratory data analysis in population data set and performed hypothesis testing over assumptions found. Used t-Test for two samples, Chi-squared Test for categorical variables, and One-way ANOVA for equality of population means through variances of samples means

Unsupervised learning

Implemented technics for customer segmentation for a credit card company and found ideal number of clusters and their main characteristics to improve service deliver model, upsell products and support strategy to market penetration. Used K-Means and Hierarchical Clustering and visualized results with dendrogram, elbow curve and silhouette score
